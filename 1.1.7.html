How does GDPR impact digital projects?

GDPR stands for the General Data Protection Regulation which is a regulation perform in all local privacy laws across the entire European Union and EEA region (European Economic Area.) It will apply to all companies selling to and storing personal information about citizens in Europe, including companies on other continents There are 8 individual rights for GDPR. These are >The right to access >The right to be forgotten >The right to data portability > The right to be informed >The right to have information corrected >The right to restrict processing >The right to object and he right to be notified. The rights to access means that each individual have the right to request access to their personal data and to ask how their data is used by the company after it has been gathered and the company has to provide a copy of the personal data, free of charge and in electronic format if requested. The rights to be forgotten means if consumers are no longer customers, or if they withdraw their consent from a company to use their personal data, t hen they have the right to have their data deleted. The rights to data portability mean people have a right to transfer their data from one service provider to another. And it must happen in a commonly used and machine-readable format. The right to be informed means any gathering of data by companies, and individuals must be informed before data is gathered. Consumers have to opt in for their data to be gathered, and consent must be freely given rather than implied. The rights to have information corrected means is ensures that individuals can have their data updated if it is out of date or incomplete or incorrect. The rights to restrict processing means Individuals can request that their data is not used for processing. Their record can remain in place, but cannot be used. The right to object means this includes the right of individuals to stop the processing of their data for direct marketing. There are no exemptions to this rule, and any processing must stop as soon as the request is received. In addition, this right must be made clear to individuals at the very start of any communication.
